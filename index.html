Hey, I'm Rowan. I'm a Computer Science student at CU Boulder graduating this spring, and I've spent the last four years building at the intersection of design and engineering. I care about making technology feel human, and most of my work reflects that in one way or another.

The project I'm most proud of is Ribbn, a nonprofit platform I founded that connects cancer patients, survivors, caregivers, and supporters through peer matching and community spaces. It's live on the App Store. Leading Ribbn has pushed me across every dimension of product work — conducting user research with people going through some of the hardest moments of their lives, designing flows in Figma, writing React Native components, managing AWS infrastructure, and navigating everything in between.

Outside of Ribbn, I work as a video editor and 3D designer at Goodles, a CPG brand, using DaVinci Resolve and Autodesk Maya for product visualizations and brand content. That creative background shapes how I approach every design problem. I think about feel, pacing, and visual tone the same way I think about component architecture and user flows.

After graduation, I'm moving to Seattle to pursue UX and product design roles, ideally at companies building in healthcare technology. The work I want to do is the kind where design decisions matter beyond conversion rates. This portfolio covers all of it — take a look around, and feel free to reach out.

Projects

Ribbn: Connect Through Cancer

Founder & CEO React Native AWS Figma 501(c)(3)

Ribbn is a nonprofit social platform I founded in 2023 to help cancer patients, survivors, and supporters find each other through peer matching, community spaces, and organization listings. The app is live on the App Store. I've led a six-person team through concept, design, development, and public beta, handling everything from Figma prototypes to React Native implementation to AWS infrastructure to legal documentation. We recently competed in CU Boulder's New Venture Challenge. Ribbn is the most complete demonstration I have of what it looks like when I care deeply about the people using it.

Specced: AI PC Configurator

Side Project Next.js TypeScript Anthropic SDK

Specced is an AI-powered PC configurator I've been building in Next.js and TypeScript using the Anthropic SDK. It differentiates from tools like PCPartPicker through a slot-based UX that guides users through the build process conversationally. The project came out of my own frustration with existing configurator tools and gave me a chance to work hands-on with LLM-integrated product design. It's still evolving, but it's a good example of how I approach building tools I actually want to use.

⚙️

Goodles — Video & 3D Design

Professional Autodesk Maya DaVinci Resolve 3D Rendering

At Goodles, I create product visualizations and brand content using Autodesk Maya and DaVinci Resolve. My work includes 3D renders of packaging, motion content for social, and edited video pieces for marketing. Working at a real CPG brand with a product in national retail has been a grounding experience — the standards for visual quality are high, and the feedback loops are fast. This work is also where my interest in 3D design and motion production is most fully developed.
